# Mosaic Wellness & Root Labs: Complete Marketing Intelligence Report

**Root Labs is a TikTok-first, gummy-only US supplement brand backed by India's fastest-growing wellness conglomerate — and it has already moved 400,000+ units with almost zero traditional brand awareness.** Mosaic Wellness, a Mumbai-based company valued at **$400 million** with **₹736 crore (~$87M) in FY25 revenue**, launched Root Labs to bring Ayurvedic ingredients like Himalayan Shilajit and KSM-66 Ashwagandha to American consumers in a modern gummy format. The brand's playbook is radically different from typical US DTC wellness launches: instead of building brand equity through PR and paid social, Root Labs has scaled almost entirely through TikTok Shop affiliates and Amazon, generating **$388K+ in TikTok GMV** and earning a "#4 in Trace Mineral Supplements" Amazon ranking — all while maintaining just **1,318 Instagram followers**. This report covers every dimension of the business, from parent company structure to ingredient-level product analysis to competitive positioning.

## The parent company: Mosaic Wellness's $400M Indian wellness empire

**Mosaic Wellness Private Limited** was incorporated in 2019 and operationally launched in May 2020 by **Revant Bhate** (CEO) and **Dhyanesh Shah**. Bhate previously served as Partner at Kstart (Kalaari Capital's seed fund) and CMO at Rebel Foods, where he built the Faasos and Behrouz Biryani brands. Shah was formerly a Principal at Eight Roads Ventures with a McKinsey consulting background. Both are IIM alumni and active angel investors.

The company is headquartered in **Thane, Maharashtra, India** and employs approximately **444 people**. Mosaic operates a multi-brand portfolio model, with each brand targeting a distinct demographic segment in India:

- **Man Matters** (launched May 2020): India's first digital men's health platform — hair loss, sexual wellness, skin, weight management. Serves **2.5M+ men annually** with 882K Instagram followers.
- **Be Bodywise** (launched May 2021): Women's health platform — PCOS, hair care, skin, nutrition. Serves **4M+ women annually** with ~1M Instagram followers.
- **Little Joys** (launched 2024): Children's health — nutrition drinks, multivitamin gummies, immunity. Serves **2M+ parents annually**.
- **Root Labs** (US market): The company's first **unisex, international brand**, described internally as "helping Indian Ayurveda reach the world."

Mosaic's financial trajectory has been remarkable. Revenue doubled from **₹333 crore in FY24 to ₹736 crore in FY25** (121% YoY growth), while net losses shrank **69%** to just ₹12 crore — placing the company at near-profitability. The company has raised approximately **$84 million** in total funding across rounds from Elevation Capital (largest external stakeholder), Peak XV Partners (formerly Sequoia India), Z47 (formerly Matrix Partners India), Think Investments ($20M Series C in April 2025 at ~$400M valuation), and most recently **360 ONE Asset** ($21M in March 2026). The founders retain **34.65%** equity, with funds holding 59.77%.

The Indian playbook that Root Labs inherits is distinctive: a **full-stack health platform** combining free telehealth consultations (150+ in-house doctors, 100K+ monthly consultations) with product sales, driving a **60% monthly repeat rate**. Marketing relies on influencer-led "vulnerability marketing" — creators sharing authentic personal struggles with taboo health topics — rather than aspirational celebrity campaigns. This approach is being adapted for the US market through TikTok creator affiliates.

## Root Labs: brand architecture, leadership, and US market positioning

Root Labs is **not a separate legal entity** but rather a brand under **Mosaic Wellness INC**, the US arm of the parent company. It operates at **rootlabs.co** (a Shopify-based DTC store) and sells across Amazon, TikTok Shop, and eBay. The brand was conceptualized and is led by **Mayank Kumar**, Founder & CEO of Root Labs and CEO of Mosaic Wellness US, who is a Wharton School graduate. Kumar's origin story — struggling with health issues during business school and finding no legitimate high-quality Ayurvedic supplement options in the US — forms the brand's founding narrative.

The brand identity was developed by **BRIGADE**, a Florence, MA-based branding agency that handled naming, positioning, packaging, website design, and copywriting. The work won a **2025 Graphic Design USA Award**. Root Labs' core positioning is captured in its tagline: **"Handpicked in Nature. Perfected in Science."** The secondary tagline is "Expand Your Roots." The brand voice strikes a clinical-premium hybrid — balancing scientific language ("ShilAbsorb Bioavailability Tech," "75% Fulvic Acid," "3.5X Higher Bioavailability") with natural, heritage-driven storytelling about Himalayan sourcing and Ayurvedic traditions.

Unlike the gender-segmented Indian brands, Root Labs is positioned as **unisex** — though its actual marketing content (energy, testosterone, stamina, vitality language; heavy male-skewing TikTok creator partnerships) leans decidedly male. The brand's social media footprint is strikingly small for its sales volume: Instagram (@rootlabsco) has only **~1,318 followers** across 179 posts. There is no meaningful presence on Facebook, YouTube, or X/Twitter. TikTok is functionally the brand's only significant social channel, but even there, the content is overwhelmingly generated by affiliate creators rather than the brand itself.

Two medical advisors lend credibility: **Dr. Douglas Christianson** (Naturopathic Doctor, Bastyr University) and **Dr. Kristy Appelhans** (NMD, MSRA, 17 years in clinical and regulatory safety). The website features an "Our Science" page focused on bioavailability and an "Honest Report" page where customers can enter batch numbers to view specific lab test certificates — a transparency play inspired by the clean-label movement.

## Target market and ideal customer profile

Root Labs' ICP emerges clearly from the intersection of its product formulations, marketing language, creator demographics, and channel strategy. The primary customer is a **25–40-year-old, digitally native American male** (despite the brand's unisex positioning) with moderate-to-high health consciousness, an interest in natural/Eastern wellness ingredients, and a preference for convenience over purity maximalism. Income skews **middle to upper-middle class** — the $27–57 monthly price point is premium relative to mass-market supplements but accessible compared to AG1 ($79/mo) or Nutrafol ($70–88/mo).

The health concerns Root Labs addresses cluster around **energy and vitality** (the dominant positioning), testosterone and hormonal support, physical stamina and performance, stress and cortisol management, immunity, cognitive clarity, and sleep quality. The brand's TikTok content and Amazon listings emphasize "Alpha" language — strength, endurance, performance — signaling a clear male fitness/wellness orientation.

Psychographically, the ICP is **Ayurveda-curious but not Ayurveda-committed**: they're open to Eastern ingredients like shilajit and ashwagandha because these are trending on social media, but they need scientific validation and modern packaging to feel comfortable purchasing. They value **clean labels** (vegan, sugar-free, non-GMO) and **transparency** (lab testing, certifications) but discover products through social proof rather than clinical research. They are **convenience-first** — the gummy format, individual wrapping, and pleasant taste are major purchase drivers. They are heavy **TikTok consumers** who trust creator reviews over brand advertising.

Without Root Labs, this customer is likely doing one of several things: taking general multivitamins or standalone ashwagandha supplements from brands like Olly or Nature's Way; consuming pure shilajit resin (messy, bitter, inconvenient); using energy drinks or excess coffee for energy; browsing AG1 or Ritual but finding them too expensive or too generic; or — importantly — **entering the supplement category for the first time** after seeing shilajit or sea moss trending on TikTok. Root Labs' TikTok-first strategy specifically captures this last cohort: the trend-driven first-time supplement buyer.

The translation from Mosaic's Indian playbook is notable. In India, Man Matters targets men aged 18–36 with a consultation-driven, full-stack health model. In the US, Root Labs strips away the telehealth layer entirely, opting for a **product-only, marketplace-heavy approach** — a pragmatic adaptation given the regulatory complexity of US telehealth and the lower brand recognition that makes consultation-based acquisition difficult.

## Competitive landscape: a narrow niche in a crowded market

Root Labs occupies a **distinctive but narrow competitive position**: it is the only notable DTC brand combining Ayurvedic heritage credibility with a shilajit-adaptogen gummy format at an accessible price point. Its competitive set bifurcates into two tiers — premium DTC wellness brands (direct competitors) and traditional supplement brands (indirect competitors) — with telehealth platforms like Hims and Ro operating in an adjacent but fundamentally different category.

**Direct competitors and how Root Labs differentiates:**

**Ritual** ($33–39/mo, capsules) leads on transparency with its "Made Traceable" visible supply chain and USP Verification — the gold standard for supplement quality. Ritual's clinical trials are published in peer-reviewed journals. Root Labs differentiates through its exotic ingredient profile (shilajit, gokshura, black musli) and gummy format versus Ritual's standard multivitamin capsules, but lacks Ritual's clinical rigor and broad product line.

**AG1/Athletic Greens** ($79–99/mo, powder) dominates the all-in-one category with NSF Certified for Sport validation, a massive influencer machine (Huberman, Rogan, Ferriss), and category-defining branding. Root Labs competes on price (roughly **60% cheaper**) and ingredient specificity rather than breadth, but cannot match AG1's brand awareness or scientific validation infrastructure.

**Nutrafol** ($70–88/mo, capsules, owned by Unilever) owns the clinical hair-growth supplement space with multiple published studies and a patented Synergen Complex. Both brands use ashwagandha and natural botanicals, but Nutrafol is exclusively hair-focused and **2–3x more expensive**. Different competitive lanes despite ingredient overlap.

**Care/of** ($7–50/mo, personalized daily packs, owned by Bayer) offers the quiz-based personalization that Root Labs entirely lacks. Care/of's breadth (dozens of individual supplements) dwarfs Root Labs' narrow catalog, but Root Labs' Ayurvedic positioning and gummy format create differentiation. Care/of competes more on convenience-of-decision than ingredient uniqueness.

**Onnit** ($38–138/mo, capsules, owned by Unilever) targets the biohacker/fitness community with Alpha Brain nootropics and Total Human daily packs. Both brands use adaptogenic ingredients and appeal to performance-minded males. Root Labs is more accessible in price and format; Onnit has deeper fitness community credibility and a broader product ecosystem.

**Seed** ($35–50/mo, capsules) occupies the premium gut-health/probiotic space with patented delivery technology and published clinical trials. Different product category entirely, but both target science-conscious wellness consumers willing to pay premium DTC prices.

**Hims & Hers** ($15–115/mo for supplements, NYSE-listed, $2.35B revenue) and **Ro/Roman** (~$20/mo for supplements) are telehealth-first platforms with **prescription medication capabilities** that Root Labs cannot replicate. They compete with Root Labs only at the OTC supplement layer, where Root Labs' Ayurvedic ingredients are highly differentiated versus their standard multivitamins.

**Indirect competitors** include traditional retail brands (Nature's Bounty, NOW Foods, Garden of Life) at **$5–40/bottle** — far cheaper but lacking DTC branding appeal — and functional beverage brands like Mud\Wtr and Four Sigmatic that overlap on adaptogenic/mushroom ingredients in different formats.

## Customer journey: TikTok discovery to Amazon conversion

The Root Labs customer journey diverges sharply from the typical DTC wellness brand playbook. Rather than a brand-website-centric funnel, the journey is **marketplace-first and creator-driven**.

**Discovery and awareness** happens overwhelmingly through **TikTok**. Root Labs partners with **Influenceish**, a TikTok Shop management agency, to run a mass micro/nano-influencer affiliate program. Over 1,000 creators produce UGC content — "Day X of 30 trying Root Labs" challenge series, "Is this a scam?!" hook reviews, unboxing videos, before/after energy testimonials, and morning routine integrations. The brand spends on **GMV Max** (TikTok's ad product) to amplify top-performing creator content and runs **MEGA livestreams** and smaller "Mini MEGA" live selling sessions. One marketing manager documented generating **$120K+ in affiliate revenue in under five months**. Total TikTok Shop GMV grew from **$36.6K to $93.9K monthly over seven months**, accumulating **$388K+ over eight months** — and the lead TikTok Shop product listing shows **400,400+ units sold** with **22,400+ reviews** at a **4.4/5 rating**.

**Category entry triggers** for the ICP include: seeing shilajit or adaptogen content trending on TikTok; experiencing energy crashes, brain fog, or stress; encountering a trusted creator's testimonial; wanting a "natural" alternative to energy drinks or pharmaceuticals; or a New Year/fitness resolution moment (Root Labs runs "New Year New Me" campaigns).

**Consideration and purchase** follows two primary paths. **Path 1 (TikTok Shop):** The customer discovers Root Labs through a creator video, clicks the product tag, reads TikTok Shop reviews (22K+), and purchases directly within TikTok — often at promotional pricing (discounts up to 77% during BFCM events). **Path 2 (Amazon):** The customer discovers Root Labs through TikTok or search, goes to Amazon for the familiar purchase environment, reads Amazon reviews, and buys — often via **Subscribe & Save** at 15% off ($27.20/mo for Alpha Gummies). **Path 3 (DTC — minority):** Some customers visit rootlabs.co directly, browse the product catalog, and purchase through Shopify checkout. There is no quiz, assessment, or personalization engine on the site — it is a straightforward product catalog.

**Post-purchase communication** is notably underdeveloped compared to mature DTC brands. The website has a newsletter signup, but there is no public evidence of sophisticated email flows, welcome sequences, post-purchase cadences, or SMS marketing. The DTC site has subscription functionality technically installed (Loop Subscriptions app) but it is **barely visible or promoted** — the Rest & Recovery product is the only one with an explicit subscription toggle, and even there the subscription price is identical to the one-time price. There is no loyalty program, no rewards system, no mobile app, and no branded community hub.

**Retention mechanics** rely primarily on: Amazon Subscribe & Save (the de facto subscription mechanism); TikTok content keeping the brand top-of-mind; product pages recommending **3–6 months of use for best results** (encouraging extended commitment); volume incentives (RL10 code for 10% off 2+ products); and product line expansion through bundles and combos. The brand appears to maintain a **Discord community** (managed through Influenceish) for creator engagement, which may extend to customers.

**The critical retention gap**: Root Labs does not own its customer relationships in a meaningful way. Sales flow primarily through Amazon (where Amazon owns the data) and TikTok Shop (where TikTok owns the data). The DTC site captures a minority of transactions. This is the brand's most significant strategic vulnerability — high sales velocity but low customer relationship depth.

## Marketing channels and the Indian playbook adapted for America

Root Labs' marketing strategy is a direct adaptation of Mosaic Wellness's proven Indian playbook, but optimized for the American platform ecosystem. In India, Man Matters pioneered **influencer-led vulnerability marketing** — creators sharing authentic personal stories about stigmatized health topics (hair loss, sexual wellness) to destigmatize the conversation and drive purchase intent. In the US, Root Labs translates this into **TikTok affiliate commerce**, where hundreds of creators share "honest review" content about energy, focus, and vitality challenges.

**TikTok Shop is the dominant channel.** The brand runs a three-pronged TikTok strategy: (1) **affiliate creator content** generating organic reach and social proof, (2) **live selling sessions** (MEGA and Mini MEGA livestreams) for real-time conversion, and (3) **GMV Max paid amplification** to scale top-performing content. Products appear under both "Root Labs" and "Be Bodywise by Root Labs" on TikTok Shop — a dual-branding approach that leverages whatever recognition Be Bodywise may have with Indian diaspora consumers.

**Amazon is the conversion engine.** Root Labs sells under the "Mosaic Wellness INC" seller account with multiple ASINs, Amazon's Choice badges, Subscribe & Save, and Amazon Live streams. The brand has achieved strong organic rankings (#4 in Trace Mineral Supplements) and "10K+ bought in past month" velocity badges that compound social proof.

**The DTC website (rootlabs.co) is a tertiary channel.** Review counts on the site are modest (10–46 per product versus 22K+ on TikTok Shop), suggesting the site captures a small fraction of total sales. The site serves more as a brand legitimacy anchor and affiliate landing page than a primary revenue driver.

**Influencer strategy is mass-micro, not celebrity.** Root Labs has no celebrity endorsements. Instead, it deploys a high-volume nano/micro-influencer affiliate model — the "Become an Affiliate" page on the website recruits creators, and TikTok Shop's open collaboration framework allows any creator to earn commissions. Content themes center on authenticity: 30-day challenge logs, skeptic-to-convert narratives ("Is this a scam?!"), and honest energy/focus testimonials. Specific creators observed include fitness enthusiasts, deal hunters, and wellness lifestyle accounts across a wide follower range.

**Press coverage is essentially nonexistent.** There are no major TechCrunch, Forbes, or mainstream wellness publication features for Root Labs. The brand has achieved scale entirely through commerce-driven channels. The only editorial mentions come from competitor-published "review" sites (NutroTonic, Tiger Shilajit, HealthInsiders) that rank for "Root Labs review" keywords — most of which redirect traffic to competing products.

**Brand voice and messaging** balances scientific authority with natural heritage. Key claims and proof points include: "4,000mg Pure Himalayan Shilajit," "ShilAbsorb™ — 3.5X Higher Bioavailability," "75%+ Fulvic Acid & 85+ Trace Minerals," "Clean Label Project Certified," "Individual Lab Certificate in Every Pack," and "100% Vegan, Sugar-Free, Non-GMO." The messaging architecture positions Root Labs as the quality answer to a flooded, low-credibility supplement market — the founder's story explicitly frames it as "finding no legitimate options" for Ayurvedic ingredients in the US.

## Why Root Labs versus competitors: the value proposition distilled

Root Labs' competitive moat rests on **five pillars**, each with varying durability:

**Ingredient heritage and sourcing story.** Root Labs is the only major US DTC brand combining authentic Indian Ayurvedic credibility with specific provenance claims — Himalayan Shilajit sourced above 16,000 feet, KSM-66 Ashwagandha from Delhi, Gokshura and Black Musli from Rajasthan. The parent company's deep roots in Indian wellness (6M+ consumers, 150+ doctors) lend authenticity that pure US brands cannot replicate. This is the brand's most durable differentiator.

**Format innovation and convenience.** The gummy-only format with individual wrapping solves real consumer pain points: shilajit resin is messy, bitter, and inconvenient; pills are unpleasant; powders require preparation. Root Labs makes ancient ingredients approachable. The ShilAbsorb™ bioavailability technology (claimed 3.5x absorption improvement) provides a proprietary technical claim, though independent clinical validation has not been publicly demonstrated.

**Price-to-value ratio.** At **$27–45/month**, Root Labs undercuts premium DTC competitors by 30–60% while maintaining premium positioning. This is likely enabled by Mosaic Wellness's Indian supply chain and manufacturing infrastructure — a structural cost advantage that most US-based competitors cannot match. The brand offers legitimate premium attributes (KSM-66 Ashwagandha, Clean Label Project certification, batch-specific COAs) at near-mass-market pricing.

**Transparency infrastructure.** The "Honest Report" batch lookup tool, individual lab certificates per pack, Clean Label Project certification, and detailed sourcing information exceed the transparency of most mass-market supplements — though they fall short of Ritual's full supply chain traceability or AG1's NSF Certified for Sport validation.

**Science and clinical backing.** This is Root Labs' weakest pillar. The brand cites KSM-66 Ashwagandha's clinical research (it is the world's most clinically studied ashwagandha extract) and general shilajit/fulvic acid studies, but **has not published proprietary clinical trials** on its finished products. The ShilAbsorb™ claim lacks publicly available independent validation. Medical advisors (Dr. Christianson, Dr. Appelhans) provide credibility but not peer-reviewed evidence. Compared to Nutrafol's published studies, AG1's NSF certification, and Seed's AFU measurement methodology, Root Labs' science story is largely borrowed from ingredient-level rather than product-level evidence.

## Conclusion: a fast-scaling brand with strategic vulnerabilities

Root Labs represents a **textbook case of Indian DTC expertise applied to the US market** — Mosaic Wellness has deployed its proven playbook of creator-driven marketing, accessible premium pricing, and Ayurvedic ingredient credibility to achieve impressive early traction. The **400K+ units moved through TikTok Shop** and **10K+ monthly Amazon purchases** demonstrate real product-market fit, particularly for the shilajit/adaptogen gummy category that is riding a major social media trend wave.

However, the brand faces four critical strategic challenges. First, its **extreme channel dependency** — TikTok Shop and Amazon own the customer relationship, leaving Root Labs vulnerable to algorithm changes, fee increases, or platform policy shifts. Building a robust DTC subscription engine with email/SMS flows, a loyalty program, and community infrastructure is the most urgent priority. Second, the **narrow product catalog** (nine SKUs in one format) limits cross-sell opportunities and customer lifetime value compared to competitors with dozens of products. Third, the **shilajit/adaptogen trend may be cyclical** — like CBD and collagen before it, ingredient-driven categories can plateau quickly, making product line diversification essential. Fourth, the **lack of proprietary clinical evidence** leaves the brand vulnerable to better-funded competitors who invest in published studies and premium third-party certifications.

The opportunity set is equally compelling. Root Labs sits at the convergence of three powerful tailwinds: the **$104B US supplement market** growing at 5.2% annually, the **Ayurvedic/adaptogen ingredient trend** gaining mainstream acceptance, and the **TikTok commerce revolution** reshaping how consumers discover and buy wellness products. Mosaic Wellness's Indian infrastructure — 150+ doctors, proven formulation expertise, cost-advantaged manufacturing, and $87M in annual revenue — provides a resource base that no other TikTok-native supplement brand can match. The question is not whether Root Labs can sell product, but whether it can build a durable brand before the trend window closes.
